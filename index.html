1. Descriptive Analytics – What Happened?

• H&M leads global transparency (71%). Zara’s brand value rose steadily, surpassing H&M by 2018.
• UK shoppers increasingly adopt sustainable behaviors (2022–2024).
• Belgium and Czechia generate the most textile waste in the EU.

2. Diagnostic Analytics – Why It Happened?

• EU sustainability laws and consumer awareness drive transparency leaders.
• Zara’s agility and online strength improved its brand value.
• COVID-19 and secondhand market growth shifted consumer habits.
• High-waste nations lack recycling infrastructure, creating new business opportunities.

3. Predictive Analytics – What Might Happen?

• Zara projected to exceed $20 B brand value by 2026; H&M may decline slightly.
• Transparency and circular-fashion policies will increase sustainability performance.
• UK sustainable behaviors expected to surpass 90% participation by 2025.

4. Recommendations

  • Invest in supply-chain transparency tools.
  • Develop recycling programs in high-waste regions.
  • Leverage resale and rental business models.
  • Communicate sustainability achievements clearly to consumers.
