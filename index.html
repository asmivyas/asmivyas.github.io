Sustainable Fashion Market Opportunity – Europe

Prepared by: Asmi Vyas | MSc Entrepreneurship | Business Intelligence | National College of Ireland

1. Descriptive Analytics – What Happened?

H&M leads in transparency (71%), followed by Calzedonia brands. Zara’s brand value grew steadily, overtaking H&M by 2018. UK consumers increasingly embrace sustainable shopping habits, while Belgium and Czechia produce the most textile waste in Europe.

2. Diagnostic Analytics – Why It Happened?

EU sustainability policies and consumer awareness drive transparency. Zara’s agile business model and digital retail strength boosted its growth. COVID-19 accelerated consumer preference for ethical and secondhand products. Countries with limited recycling infrastructure generate higher textile waste, indicating a market gap for circular innovation.

3. Predictive Analytics – What Might Happen?

Zara’s brand value is projected to exceed $20B by 2026, while H&M’s growth may plateau. Transparency scores are expected to rise due to EU textile regulations. UK sustainable consumption could surpass 90% participation by 2025.

4. Recommendations

  • Invest in traceable and transparent supply-chain systems.
  • Expand recycling and upcycling programs in high-waste countries.
  • Adopt circular business models such as resale and rentals.
  • Communicate sustainability performance to strengthen brand trust.
